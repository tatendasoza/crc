About Our Story

Welcome to Faith Ministries City Restoration Centre

Brief From History

One Sunday in December, 1999 at Mbare Community Church (MCC) Faith Ministries, Pastor N Mnkandla challenged the Mount Pleasant Home group to start a congregation in the northern suburbs. This was to spread the gospel of Jesus Christ to all in the northern suburbs.

Our Mission

Our mission is to disciple people through developing relationships with them. Matthew 28:19, Acts 2:42

    THE PRAYERS

    The members took the challenge to God in prayer and sought for wisdom and guidance. Prayer meetings were held every Sunday afternoon and prayer drives were also carried out in the proposed neighbourhood. Some of the prayer issues amongst many others were a pastor, offices, praise and worship team, venue for Sunday meetings, teachers for children’s church, chairs, transport for chairs and instruments.

    The Launch

    The name of the congregation was agreed as Faith Ministries Borrowdale Community Church (BCC). The home group members plus others who felt they could partner in extending the kingdom of God were prayed out of MCC on Sunday 9th July 2000. The church begun meeting on the 16th of July 2000 at GGs restaurant housed at the Borrowdale racecourse. The following home groups were enacted at inception, City, Mt Pleasant, Greystone Park, and Chisipite. Faith Ministries Epworth Congregation donated the first set of musical instruments. The AWANA (Approved Workmen Are Not Ashamed) children’s programme based on 2 Timothy 2 verse 15 was started at inception and run from rented premises like 28 Oxford Road, Newlands where the congregation at one time had offices and United Methodist Church (UMC) in Chisipite, Harare till the congregation moved onsite to now Stand 492 Harare Drive in 2003

    THE FIRST SERMON

    The inaugural service on 16th July 2000 was conducted by Pastor Mnkandla, now Bishop Mnkandla on a chilly July morning. The message was based on Matthew 4: 18 – 19 with emphasis on follow me and I will make you fishers of men. Pastor stated that BCC was a journey of adventure however the One who calls us is faithful. The disciples were diligent, had relationships and were hardworking businessmen. All members were called to serve and to be like Jesus. The prophetic word was, “I will make you fishers of men, and He who has began a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ. God takes the ordinary and makes it extra ordinary and He takes the natural and makes it Supernatural”. A banner inscribed, “At the foot of the cross the ground is level”, for there is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is neither male nor female, circumcised nor uncircumcised, but Christ is all and in all was hanging upfront. Tea and eats was served at the end.

    THE JOURNEY

    The congregation met at the GG restaurant up until about December 2000 when it moved venue to the Reps Theatre on Second Street extension in Avondale. BCC was the first church to use the facility as a place of worship for the first time in seventy years of the theatre coming into existence. All glory and honour to God. When the venue was in use on some Sundays, services were held at Belgravia Sports Club. The disruptions and inconveniences which used to occur during Sunday services forced the eldership to move church to 492 Harare Drive sometime in 2003 when the building and the car park were not ready. All things work together for good as the congregation saved on rentals.

    THE PASTORATE

    Two stands on Harare Drive, Borrowdale were secured in 2000/2001 and paid for in cash. The two stands were then consolidated into Stand 492, Harare Drive in 2002 as a place of assembly. It is of worth to mention that under the foundation of the building a BIBLE was planted by the Senior Pastor. A small core group of people was involved centrally and funded most of the work. No borrowings were involved in the whole construction of the building.

    THE TARGET

    The main target of BCC in the Northern Suburbs was placed into two groups, namely “Mr and Mrs Nyakudenga”, i.e. those people who had had an encounter with Christ, love the Lord and want to go to heaven and “Mr and Mrs Nyakuziva”, i.e. the educated and professionals who live within their durawalled properties, are conscious of time and have a choice of attending church or watching gospel on satellite television, the diplomats, senior government officials and corporate executives and their families. Mr and Mrs Nyakuziva were the main target in terms of evangelism.
